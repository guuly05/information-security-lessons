# Podcasts & YouTube Channels

## Learn Cybersecurity on the Go

This page curates the best audio and video resources for staying current with cybersecurity news, learning new techniques, and understanding the human stories behind the headlines. Whether you're commuting, exercising, or studying, these resources will help you stay sharp.

---

## 🎙️ Podcasts

### Security Now!

![Security Now Logo](https://twit.tv/sites/default/files/styles/logo_600x600/public/security_now_0.png?itok=3KkG8Uvs)

**Hosts:** Steve Gibson & Leo Laporte
**Network:** TWiT (This Week in Tech)
**Frequency:** Weekly (every Tuesday)

> *"Cybersecurity guru Steve Gibson joins Leo Laporte every Tuesday. Steve and Leo break down the latest cybercrime and hacking stories, offering a deep understanding of what's happening and how to protect yourself and your business."*

**About:** Security Now! has been running since summer 2005, making it one of the longest-running cybersecurity podcasts. Steve Gibson, who coined the term "spyware" and created the first anti-spyware program, provides deep technical insights into security vulnerabilities, exploits, and defensive strategies.

**Why Listen:**
- Deep technical dives into security vulnerabilities and exploits
- Clear explanations of complex security concepts
- Regular Q\&A episodes where listener questions are answered
- Insights from a true pioneer in the field

**Best For:** Students who want to understand the technical details of security threats and defenses.

**Start With:** Any episode—topics are current and standalone. The Q\&A episodes are particularly accessible for beginners.

**Where to Listen:** [TWiT.tv](https://twit.tv/shows/security-now), Apple Podcasts, Spotify, YouTube

---

### Darknet Diaries

![Darknet Diaries Logo](https://darknetdiaries.com/img/cover_art.jpg)

**Host:** Jack Rhysider
**Frequency:** Bi-weekly (every two weeks)

> *"Darknet Diaries, hosted by Jack Rhysider, chronicles the darker sides of the internet."*

**About:** Darknet Diaries tells true stories from the darker side of the internet—hackers, breaches, cybercrime, espionage, and the people who investigate them. Each episode features interviews with hackers, security professionals, law enforcement, and sometimes even the victims of cybercrime.

**Why Listen:**
- Engaging storytelling that brings security concepts to life
- Real-world case studies of breaches, hacks, and investigations
- Interviews with people who were actually there
- Accessible to beginners while still interesting for experts

**Best For:** Understanding the human side of cybersecurity, real-world attack narratives, and building context for the technical concepts you're learning.

**Start With:** Episode 1 (or any episode—each is self-contained). Popular episodes include:
- Episode 50: "The Beirut Bank Job" (ATM hacking)
- Episode 100: "The Hacker Mindset" (interviews with hackers)
- The "Xbox Underground" series (console hacking)

**Where to Listen:** [darknetdiaries.com](https://darknetdiaries.com), Apple Podcasts, Spotify, YouTube

---

### Smashing Security

**Hosts:** Graham Cluley & Carole Theriault
**Frequency:** Weekly

This podcast takes a lighter, more conversational approach to cybersecurity news. Graham and Carole (both former Sophos security experts) discuss the latest security news, breaches, and weird stories from the world of tech.

**Why Listen:**
- Approachable and entertaining
- Covers current security news in a digestible format
- Great for staying current without feeling overwhelmed

**Best For:** Staying up to date with security news in an accessible format.

**Where to Listen:** [smashingsecurity.com](https://www.smashingsecurity.com), Apple Podcasts, Spotify

---

### The CyberWire Daily Briefing

**Host:** Dave Bittner
**Frequency:** Daily

A short (10-15 minute) daily podcast that covers the most important cybersecurity news. Perfect for busy students who want to stay current without a large time commitment.

**Why Listen:**
- Quick, daily updates on security news
- Covers policy, threats, and industry developments
- Includes a "research" segment with deeper dives

**Best For:** Daily news consumption and staying aware of the broader security landscape.

**Where to Listen:** [thecyberwire.com](https://thecyberwire.com), Apple Podcasts, Spotify

## 📺 YouTube Channels

### John Hammond

![John Hammond YouTube](https://yt3.googleusercontent.com/ytc/APkrFKaZWlPkI1pPP0n6lZ2jMz5-HbO5gU-WrVRVILQ=s900-c-k-c0x00ffffff-no-rj)

**Channel:** [John Hammond on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/JohnHammond010)

> *"Hacking, CTFs, and security challenges."*

**About:** John Hammond is a former US Air Force cyber warfare operator who creates engaging, educational content focused on Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions, penetration testing, and cybersecurity training. His videos are fast-paced, practical, and accessible.

**Why Watch:**
- Walkthroughs of CTF challenges
- Practical penetration testing demonstrations
- Clear explanations of security concepts
- Engaging presentation style

**Best For:** Hands-on learners who want to see security techniques in action.

**Start With:** His CTF walkthrough series or beginner-friendly penetration testing videos.

---

### NetworkChuck

**Channel:** [NetworkChuck on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/NetworkChuck)

> *"Perfect for beginners and intermediate learners. He makes cybersecurity, networking, Linux, and cloud feel simple and exciting."*

**About:** NetworkChuck creates high-energy, engaging content that makes complex technical topics accessible. His videos cover networking, cybersecurity, Linux, cloud computing, and career development.

**Why Watch:**
- Engaging and motivational presentation style
- Clear explanations for beginners
- Covers a wide range of topics
- Includes career advice and certification guidance

**Best For:** Beginners who want to build foundational knowledge in an engaging format.

---

### The Cyber Mentor

**Channel:** [The Cyber Mentor on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/TheCyberMentor)

> *"Ethical hacking and penetration testing."*

**About:** Heath Adams (The Cyber Mentor) is a penetration tester who creates comprehensive, structured training content. His videos cover everything from basic networking to advanced exploitation techniques.

**Why Watch:**
- Structured, course-like content
- Practical penetration testing demonstrations
- Certification-focused content (including his own Practical Ethical Hacking course)
- Real-world scenarios and examples

**Best For:** Students working toward penetration testing certifications or careers.

---

### HackerSploit

**Channel:** [HackerSploit on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/HackerSploit)

> *"Ethical hacking tutorials and cybersecurity."*

**About:** HackerSploit provides detailed tutorials on ethical hacking, penetration testing, and cybersecurity fundamentals. The channel covers tools like Kali Linux, Metasploit, Nmap, and Burp Suite.

**Why Watch:**
- Detailed, step-by-step tutorials
- Focus on practical tools and techniques
- Covers both offensive and defensive security
- Regular updates with new content

**Best For:** Learning specific tools and techniques through detailed walkthroughs.

---

### Hak5

**Channel:** [Hak5 on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/Hak5)

**About:** Hak5 is one of the longest-running cybersecurity channels on YouTube. It covers a wide range of topics including hardware hacking, network security, and penetration testing tools.

**Why Watch:**
- Covers both software and hardware security
- Reviews and demonstrates security tools
- Long-running, established reputation
- Entertaining and informative

**Best For:** Students interested in both software and hardware security, and seeing security tools in action.

---

### David Bombal

**Channel:** [David Bombal on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/DavidBombal)

**About:** David Bombal creates content focused on networking, cybersecurity, and certification training. His videos often feature interviews with industry experts and deep dives into specific technologies.

**Why Watch:**
- Expert interviews and insights
- Deep technical dives
- Certification-focused content
- Practical demonstrations

**Best For:** Understanding networking fundamentals and their security implications.

---

### IppSec

**Channel:** [IppSec on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/ippsec)

> *"The Penetration Testing Guru."*

**About:** IppSec creates detailed walkthroughs of Hack The Box (HTB) machines, explaining the thought process behind each step of a penetration test.

**Why Watch:**
- Detailed, methodical walkthroughs
- Explains the "why" behind each step
- Realistic penetration testing scenarios
- Excellent for learning methodology

**Best For:** Students preparing for penetration testing certifications or those who want to understand the penetration testing methodology.

## 💡 Study Tips

1. **Combine Formats:** Pair podcasts (passive learning) with YouTube (visual learning) and books (deep reading)
2. **Take Notes:** Keep a digital or physical notebook of key concepts and interesting techniques
3. **Follow Along:** Many YouTube channels provide labs and exercises—don't just watch, do
4. **Engage with the Community:** Join Discord servers, subreddits, and forums to discuss what you're learning
5. **Apply What You Learn:** Set up a home lab and practice the techniques you see
